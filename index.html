Services.

The Dominican Women’s Development Center (DWDC) is a non-profit organization located in Washington Heights, Northern Manhattan. DWDC was founded in 1988 by nine Dominican women who identified the need for Latinas to organize around critical issues and seek solutions to systemic problems affecting their lives. DWDC serves more than 9,000 families through outreach, community education, and direct services annually in our Early Head Start, Health Promotions, Childcare & Learning Center, New Dawn Anti-Domestic Violence, Healthy Families, and Education Development Programs

The Dominican Women’s Development Center’s Health Promotion Program envisions health as life. Health is central to the development of every person and community. Building and promoting the health and wellness of our community is necessary for fostering a more equitable and just world. We aim to serve as a resource for community members to make conscious decisions for themselves and their families in regards to their health and wellness and to create solutions together in addressing health disparities in the area.

Programs.

DWDC is committed to furthering Dominican Women in the Washington Heights area. We have a wide variety of programs that include sexual health and education, family development, and anti-domestic violence programs.

Our programs include:

Education

This adult literacy program serves more than 400Spanish speaking students annually.The goal is to prepare students to enroll in college, prepare for careerreadiness and/or secure living wage employment.

Family

We offer many services to families in our area, inluding The Healthy Families Washington Heights Program for new parents, Mama Tingo Childcare programs for children of all ages, and anti-domestic violence programs that aim to empower women to gain control of their lives.
